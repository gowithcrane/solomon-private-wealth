What "AI Readiness" actually means

AI Readiness isn't about whether you use ChatGPT. It's about whether your practice is set up to actually benefit from AI – so it can save you time, reduce key-person risk, and give you leverage on the work you're already doing, without creating compliance exposure.

For that to happen, five things need to be in place:

  • Your data is usable. AI can't help if your client information lives in someone's head.
  • You know where your time is going. Repetitive tasks you can name are tasks you can delegate.
  • You know your clients. Who they are, what they want, and how they came to you. AI can't help you serve people you don't understand.
  • There's a clear way you do things. AI amplifies what's repeatable. If your methods live in one person's head, there's nothing to amplify yet.
  • You have momentum. You've tried a few things, you're curious, and you want to move – within the guardrails your industry requires.

The five conditions below are how we read where Solomon is today. The bigger the gap on any one condition, the more leverage a small change can unlock.
